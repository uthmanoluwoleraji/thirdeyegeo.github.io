M80 Ring Road – Groundwater & Geotechnical Assessment

Sector: Transport infrastructure  •  Role: Engineering Geologist / Hydrogeologist

Supervised drilling and test pitting, logged ground conditions and coordinated groundwater monitoring to support design of cuttings and drainage. Integrated geotechnical and groundwater data into a clear conceptual model for designers, highlighting seepage risks and appropriate foundation solutions.

View related LinkedIn post

Open Pit Dewatering – Cone of Depression & Seepage Face

Sector: Mining  •  Focus: Pumping test interpretation & inflow assessment

Analysed pumping test data to estimate transmissivity and drawdown behaviour around an open pit, including development of a seepage face at the excavation wall. Clarified implications for groundwater inflow, pit wall performance and dewatering system design.

View technical LinkedIn post

Contaminant Transport – Ogata–Banks Breakthrough Curves

Sector: Contaminated land / Hydrogeology  •  Focus: Advection–dispersion analysis

Used the Ogata–Banks solution to evaluate contaminant migration under varying velocity and dispersivity conditions. Presented breakthrough curves to illustrate advection- and dispersion- dominated regimes, helping stakeholders understand plume behaviour and monitoring requirements.

View modelling LinkedIn post

Dam & Spring Protection – Hydrogeological Conceptual Model

Sector: Water / Environmental  •  Focus: EPBC-listed springs & impact assessment

Developed a hydrogeological conceptual model for a proposed dam located within a basin containing springs of national environmental significance. Assessed potential drawdown pathways and designed a low-risk configuration aimed at achieving “no impact” to spring discharge.

About Third Eye Geoscience

Third Eye Geoscience & Subsurface Consulting is led by an engineering geologist and hydrogeologist with experience across major Australian infrastructure, mining and land development projects.

With a background spanning geophysics, engineering geology and groundwater hydrology, the practice specialises in connecting field investigation data with clear, defensible interpretations that designers and decision-makers can rely on.

Experience includes freeway upgrades, rail corridor projects, tunnels, open pits, land development and environmental assessments. Tools commonly used include borehole logging, instrumentation (piezometers, VWPs, dataloggers), pumping test analysis, analytical solutions, 3D geological modelling and seepage/inflow assessments.

Core strengths:
• Translating complex ground and groundwater behaviour into clear, concise reporting
• Integrating geological, geotechnical and hydrogeological data
• Collaborating with designers, contractors and regulators to de-risk subsurface uncertainty.
